About me

I'm a Senior Data Scientist x Product leader based in Edinburgh. My career has taken me from developing medical imaging algorithms at the University of Edinburgh, to building a B2B SaaS medical device as Product Manager at a Scottish Edge-winning medtech startup, to working in translation consulting.

I've been working with AI since 2017, specialising in computer vision, medical imaging, and data-driven products. Along the way, I've led cross-functional agile teams, contributed to FDA 510(k) pre-submissions, implemented generative AI features that reduced data collection costs, and helped shape products from early concept through to investor-ready roadmaps. I particularly enjoy building confidence and capability within teams, helping people do their best work while delivering ambitious technical projects collaboratively.

Outside of work, I'm usually renovating my home, walking my dog, playing guitar, or writing fiction. I'm currently writing the Blood Without Dawn trilogy, a dark fantasy series. I'm also a maker at heart, check out my creative projects below!

Product Management & Entrepreneurship

Eye to the Future

From research to medical device, Eye to the Future was developing advanced retinal image analysis as a clinical decision support tool for optometrists. My role as product manager involved aligning cross functional teams, developing product strategy, establishing company wide regulatory compliance, and being the voice of the customer in product development. I also designed the UX, including a novel image navigation feature to save optometrists time. Common feedback when using this feature was "Ooooo!".

AI Research & Development

Academic Researcher

During my PhD and Post-doc at the University of Edinburgh I built AI pipelines for retinal image and clinical data analysis. Working across ophthalmology and data science, I developed automated tools to detect age-related macular degeneration and discovered novel retinal vascular biomarkers linked to Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease. I was also a member of the SCONe team, establishing Scotland's largest community retinal image repository spanning optometry practices nationwide, enabling population-scale disease prediction research.
